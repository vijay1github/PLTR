WONDERS OF WORLD

The Seven Wonders of the World encompass remarkable man-made structures celebrated for their historical, architectural, and cultural significance.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Completed in 1653, it is renowned for its architectural beauty, intricate carvings, and symmetrical design, symbolizing eternal love.

Eiffle Tower

The Eiffel Tower, located in Paris, France, is an iconic wrought-iron lattice tower constructed for the 1889 World's Fair by engineer Gustave Eiffel. Standing at 324 meters tall, it was the tallest man-made structure in the world until 1930.

China Wall

The Great Wall of China is an ancient series of walls and fortifications, totaling over 13,000 miles, built to protect Chinese states and empires from invasions. Constructed between the 7th century BC and the 16th century AD, it showcases remarkable engineering and strategic military planning.
